## The Problem

WeChat official account articles are notoriously difficult for NotebookLM to import:
- You paste the link, and NotebookLM shows "imported successfully"
- But when you open it, the content is an empty WeChat verification page -- a **fake success**

## How Jetpack Handles It

### How It Works

WeChat articles require full browser rendering to load their content. Jetpack's approach:

1. Open the article in the browser and wait for JavaScript rendering to complete
2. Extract the body content from the rendered DOM
3. Import into NotebookLM as plain text, bypassing the link-based import limitation

### Import Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the article in your browser">
    Copy the article link from WeChat and open it in Chrome. Make sure the page content displays properly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Import with Jetpack">
    Click the Jetpack icon and import the current page. The extension extracts the fully rendered body content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Fixing Fake Successes

If you previously imported WeChat articles using NotebookLM's built-in import (resulting in fake successes):

1. Open the NotebookLM notebook page
2. Jetpack automatically detects fake-success sources
3. A **blue banner** appears at the top of the page
4. Click "Repair" to batch fix all of them at once

<Warning>
  WeChat article links can expire over time and may become inaccessible. Import them sooner rather than later.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  **Batch workflow**: Bookmark multiple WeChat articles in Jetpack first, then aggregate and export as PDF to upload to NotebookLM -- much more efficient.
</Tip>
