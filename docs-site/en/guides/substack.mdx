## Why Does NotebookLM's Direct Import Fall Short with Substack?

Importing a Substack link directly through NotebookLM typically leads to:
- Anti-scraping mechanisms blocking the import entirely
- A "successful" import that includes tons of noise: Subscribe buttons, comment sections, recommended articles, footers

## How Jetpack Handles It

Jetpack has purpose-built optimizations for Substack articles:

### 14 Noise Filters

The following elements are automatically filtered out:
- Subscribe buttons and popups
- Comment sections
- Recommended article lists
- Author bio cards
- Social sharing buttons
- Header and footer navigation
- Paywall overlays (free portion is still extracted)
- Ads and sponsored content

### Import Methods

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Method 1: Single article import">
    Paste a Substack article link into Jetpack and click import. The extension automatically extracts the body text.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Method 2: RSS batch import">
    Substack natively supports RSS. Paste `yourname.substack.com/feed` into RSS Import, select articles, and batch import.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Method 3: Bookmark and aggregate">
    Bookmark interesting Substack articles one by one, then aggregate and export as PDF, and upload to NotebookLM. Great for compiling a series of posts by the same author.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  **Paywalled articles**: Jetpack can only extract the publicly visible portion. If you're a paid subscriber, log in to the browser first before importing to get the full content.
</Tip>
